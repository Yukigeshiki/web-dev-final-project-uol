Report: Large Coursework

Introduction

In my website I have tried to convey a sense of warm friendliness. I have attempted to acheive this by making UI that doesn't confuse or overpower the users senses. I've kept the layout as minimal and clean as possible without it seeming bland or empty of content. I have gone with a Hierarchical Model as I felt this was the best way to acheive a sense of ease while moving through the site; I've tried to make sure that the user has full control over where they would like to navigate without being pushed towards any one area.

State 3 things that have inspired you when creating your website

  1. First and foremost I was inspired by my lifelong love of succulent plants.
  2. Secondly, my love for minimal and clean design - not just with regards to websites, but in all aspects of art and design.
  3. Lastly, I was inspired by my hope that I will eventually be able to create a production ready website for my succulent plants.

State 3 ways in which your site is accessible

  1. I have tried to lay out all links in a well spread out and easy to navigate manner when using only a keyboard.
  2. I have made every attempt at making the site visually appealing and easy to navigate on screens of any size.
  3. I have used a color scheme that I hope will not cause those who are visually impaired or color blind to struggle when viewing the site.

State 3 ways in which you have considered usability in your site

  1. I have attempted to keep the design clean from unnecessary distractions so the user can navigate the site with ease.
  2. Instead of inlining large pieces of text, I have given the user the opportunity to open the text in a new tab. This prevents the site from becoming crowded and making it difficult for a user's eyes to navigate.
  3. I have tried to separate out each piece of subject matter, eg. events, so that the user can easily find what they are looking for without having to sift through unrelated items.

State 3 things you had to learn/find out by yourself when creating your site, and describe how you did that

  1. I had to learn how to access a javascript object holding data within my HTML file, and then pass pieces of that data into another javascript function as a paramater. I learnt this by searching google for articles and code examples relating to how HTML and javascript interact.
  2. I had to learn how to open an HTML file and then write data to it using a javascript function, depending on what link was clicked. I learnt how to do this by researching aspects of the DOM using google.
  3. I had to learn how to change my top nav items into a drop down menu when the screen became small. This I did by adapting the W3Schools menu to suit my sites needs.

What aspect(s) of your work do you think worked well and why?

A few come to mind; first of all I really enjoyed how the images helped to light up the pages and make my attempt at a minimal design seem less dull. Other than that I was really glad that the inserted videos worked out nicely. I feel they add an extra something that the site wouldn't have without them. Lastly, I think the welcome page added to the overall feel of the site by starting the user on a clean, bright note.

What aspect(s) of your work could be improved, and how might you do things differently another time?

Next time round I would definitely put more thought into how my designs can be implemented rather than just designing and hoping that the implementation will just work without any problems. Also, now that I have had experience attempting to make my site fully responsive there are aspects that I would redesign in a way that made responsiveness easier to achieve. There are also Javascript elements that I had planned but had to leave out because I ran out of time; because of what I have learnt this time round, I feel implementing them would now be possible. I have tried my best to provide meaningful content but again ran out of time - knowing what I know now about how long certain things took to implement, I may have changed out certain things that were content heavy for something else. This would be to minimise the amount of dummy content I landed up having to use.

What resources were used when creating your work?

I used my store of photographs of my plants for all images. I used a mix of the W3Schools website, Code Pen website, Stack Overflow and Medium articles for research and inspiration pertaining to HTML, UI/UX design, Javascript and CSS. Items taken from other sources were the main setup for my CSS, the drop down menu used for the on smaller screens, the slideshow, and the login/signup pages. The CSS was taken from Udemy and adapted to fit the needs of my page. The drop down menu was taken from W3School and was modified by making all nav items dissapear when the screen is too small to fit each item (the original still showed the home nav item). I also changed all styling to fit with the theme of my site. The slideshow was also modified from code found on W3School. In this case I removed all the extras as I wanted them to fit into the minimal design of the site. The styling was also drastically changed to make it more bold and to fill the extent of the screen width. The login/signup page was taken from Code Pen. I removed all the javascript as I didn't think I would find the time to hook it up to anything worth while. I also changed the styling to fit in with the site and I added javascript code so that the Login nav item changed to Logout when the login button is clicked.
